STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE — WIRE TRANSFER PROCESSING
Document ID: SOP-OPS-001 | Version: 3.2 | Classification: Internal
Department: Banking Operations | Owner: Operations SME Team
Last Updated: January 2025

==============================================================================
1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE
==============================================================================

This SOP outlines the end-to-end process for initiating, verifying, and completing
domestic and international wire transfers within Citi Banking Operations. It applies
to all Operations Analysts (L1-L3), Team Leads, and Compliance staff involved in
wire transfer processing.

==============================================================================
2. WIRE TRANSFER INITIATION — DOMESTIC (USD)
==============================================================================

2.1 Pre-requisites
- Initiator must be an authorized wire transfer operator (WTO) with active Flexcube access
- Client must have a verified and active demand deposit account (DDA)
- Daily wire limit: $500,000 per transaction for standard operators; $2,000,000 for senior operators
- Transactions above $10,000 automatically trigger FinCEN CTR reporting requirements

2.2 Step-by-Step Process

Step 1: Log in to Flexcube Core Banking System using SSO credentials
Step 2: Navigate to Transactions > Wire Transfer > New Domestic Wire
Step 3: Enter beneficiary details:
   - Full legal name (must match account holder name exactly)
   - ABA routing number (9 digits)
   - Account number (up to 17 digits)
   - Payment amount in USD
   - Memo / Reference field (mandatory for amounts > $50,000)
Step 4: Run OFAC sanctions screening — system auto-triggers, do NOT proceed if flagged
Step 5: Verify dual-control approval for transactions > $100,000
   - Second approver must be at Team Lead level or above
   - Approval must be completed within 30 minutes of initiation
Step 6: Submit and retain the Flexcube confirmation number (format: DCW-YYYYMMDD-XXXXXXXX)
Step 7: Send confirmation email to client within 15 minutes of successful submission

2.3 Cut-off Times
- Fedwire: 5:30 PM ET (Monday–Friday, excluding Federal holidays)
- CHIPS: 4:45 PM ET
- Post-deadline transactions are processed next business day

==============================================================================
3. WIRE TRANSFER INITIATION — INTERNATIONAL (SWIFT)
==============================================================================

3.1 Additional Requirements for International Wires

- Beneficiary Bank SWIFT/BIC code (8 or 11 characters)
- Beneficiary IBAN (for EU transactions) or account number
- Correspondent bank details if required
- Currency conversion rate confirmation from FX Desk (for non-USD)
- Purpose of payment (mandatory per regulatory requirements)

3.2 SWIFT Message Types
- MT103: Customer credit transfer (most common)
- MT202: Financial institution transfer
- MT199: Free-format message for queries

3.3 Compliance Checks for International Wires
- OFAC SDN List screening (automatic)
- EU Sanctions screening (automatic for EUR transactions)
- PEP (Politically Exposed Person) check for amounts > $25,000
- Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD) for high-risk countries (see Appendix A)

==============================================================================
4. EXCEPTION HANDLING
==============================================================================

4.1 OFAC Sanctions Hit
- Do NOT release the transaction
- Immediately escalate to Compliance team (compliance-alert@citi.com)
- Place the transaction in "Pending Compliance Review" status in Flexcube
- Response required within 4 business hours
- Document all actions in the wire transfer log

4.2 Beneficiary Mismatch
- If the beneficiary name does not exactly match the account records:
  - For minor discrepancies (typos, abbreviations): Team Lead can approve with notes
  - For significant discrepancies: Reject and return funds to originator
  - Never process a wire with a completely different beneficiary name

4.3 Insufficient Funds
- System will auto-reject if DDA balance is insufficient
- Notify client within 1 business hour
- Do not allow overdraft for wire transfers (unlike check processing)

4.4 Duplicate Wire Detection
- System flags potential duplicates (same amount, same beneficiary, within 24 hours)
- Analyst must call client to confirm before releasing duplicate
- Log duplicate verification in the wire transfer audit trail

==============================================================================
5. REGULATORY REPORTING
==============================================================================

5.1 Currency Transaction Reports (CTR)
- Required for any cash transaction exceeding $10,000 in a single business day
- Filed with FinCEN within 15 calendar days
- System auto-generates CTR draft; analyst must review and confirm within 24 hours

5.2 Suspicious Activity Reports (SAR)
- Required when suspicious activity is detected (structuring, unusual patterns)
- Must be filed within 30 days of detecting suspicious activity
- Do not notify the customer that a SAR has been filed (tipping-off prohibition)
- Escalate to BSA/AML team via secure portal

5.3 Record Retention
- Wire transfer records must be retained for 7 years per BSA requirements
- Records include: initiation details, approvals, OFAC results, client confirmations

==============================================================================
6. SYSTEM ACCESS AND CONTROLS
==============================================================================

- Flexcube access reviewed quarterly; unused accounts deactivated after 90 days
- All wire transfer activity logged with timestamp, user ID, and IP address
- Segregation of duties: initiator ≠ approver for transactions > $100,000
- Supervisory override requires dual authorization from Operations Director level
