1. POLICY OVERVIEW
==============================================================================

This policy establishes Know Your Customer (KYC) and Anti-Money Laundering (AML)
requirements for Citi Banking Operations. Compliance with this policy is mandatory
for all staff who handle customer onboarding, account management, and transaction
monitoring.

This document is CONFIDENTIAL and restricted to Compliance and Operations teams only.

==============================================================================
2. CUSTOMER DUE DILIGENCE (CDD)
==============================================================================

2.1 Standard CDD Requirements (All Customers)

Individual Customers:
- Government-issued photo ID (passport, driver's license, national ID)
- Proof of address (utility bill, bank statement — not older than 3 months)
- Social Security Number (SSN) or Tax Identification Number (TIN)
- Date of birth verification

Business Customers:
- Certificate of Incorporation or equivalent formation document
- Articles of Association / Operating Agreement
- EIN (Employer Identification Number)
- Proof of business address
- List of authorized signatories with ID for each
- Beneficial Ownership Form (FinCEN CDD Rule): all individuals owning ≥ 25% equity

2.2 Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD)

EDD is required for:
- Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs) and their immediate family members
- Customers from FATF high-risk or monitored jurisdictions (see current FATF list)
- Correspondent banking relationships
- Private banking customers with assets > $1 million
- Non-profit organizations receiving international funds
- Shell companies or complex ownership structures

EDD Process:
1. Complete standard CDD documentation
2. Obtain source of funds declaration (written, signed)
3. Conduct adverse media screening (use WorldCheck or equivalent)
4. Senior Management approval required before account opening
5. Document EDD findings in CRM with supporting evidence
6. Ongoing monitoring frequency: quarterly (vs. annual for standard CDD)

3. CUSTOMER RISK RATING
==============================================================================

Risk Category | Score Range | Review Frequency | Monitoring Level
-------------|-------------|-----------------|----------------
Low Risk     | 0–30        | Annual          | Standard automated
Medium Risk  | 31–60       | Semi-annual     | Enhanced automated
High Risk    | 61–80       | Quarterly       | Manual + automated
Very High    | 81–100      | Monthly         | Dedicated analyst

Risk scoring factors:
- Country of origin / jurisdiction (weighted 30%)
- Industry / business type (weighted 20%)
- Transaction volume and complexity (weighted 25%)
- PEP status (weighted 15%)
- Adverse media hits (weighted 10%)

==============================================================================
4. TRANSACTION MONITORING
==============================================================================

4.1 Automated Monitoring Rules (Actimize AML Platform)

Rule ID | Description | Threshold | Action
--------|-------------|-----------|-------
TM-001  | Large cash deposits | > $9,000 | Alert for analyst review
TM-002  | Structuring detection | Multiple transactions < $10K in 24h | SAR investigation
TM-003  | Rapid fund movement | Funds in/out within 48 hours | Alert
TM-004  | Unusual wire patterns | 3+ international wires in 7 days | EDD trigger
TM-005  | Dormant account activity | No activity 12 months then large transaction | Alert
TM-006  | High-risk country wire | Any wire to FATF blacklist country | Mandatory hold + compliance review

4.2 Alert Handling Process

Level 1 (Analyst): Review alert context, customer history, prior SARs
- Clear if explainable by customer profile and history
- Escalate to Level 2 if suspicious patterns confirmed

Level 2 (Senior Analyst / Team Lead): Deep investigation
- Review 90-day transaction history
- Check adverse media, PEP databases
- Interview relationship manager if available
- Decision: Clear, SAR filing, or account restriction

Level 3 (BSA/AML Team): SAR filing and regulatory escalation
- File SAR with FinCEN within 30 days
- Account restrictions or closure as appropriate
- Coordinate with law enforcement if required

5. HIGH-RISK JURISDICTIONS
==============================================================================

FATF Blacklist (Immediate Restrictions — all transactions require compliance pre-approval):
North Korea, Iran, Myanmar

FATF Grey List (Enhanced Monitoring Required):
Bulgaria, Burkina Faso, Cameroon, Croatia, Democratic Republic of Congo,
Haiti, Kenya, Mali, Mozambique, Namibia, Nigeria, Philippines, Senegal,
South Africa, Syria, Tanzania, Trinidad and Tobago, Vietnam, Yemen

Internal Watch List (Additional Citi restrictions — see Internal Policy Portal):
- Subject to quarterly update
- Access requires Compliance role credentials

==============================================================================
6. RECORD KEEPING
==============================================================================

- CDD documents: 5 years from account closure
- SAR filings: 5 years from filing date
- Transaction records: 7 years (BSA requirement)
- EDD records: 7 years
- All records must be retrievable within 48 hours for regulatory requests

==============================================================================
7. TRAINING AND CERTIFICATION
==============================================================================

- Annual AML/KYC training mandatory for all Operations and Compliance staff
- BSA certification required for analysts processing high-risk transactions
- New joiners: must complete KYC fundamentals module within first 30 days
- Non-compliance with training requirements triggers access restriction to Flexcube
